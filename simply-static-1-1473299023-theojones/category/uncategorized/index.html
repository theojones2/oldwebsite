Misleading Headlines

In today’s episode of misleading headlines, we have Huff Po’s front page “SCOTUS SPARES ROE v. WADE Court Rules 5-3 Against TX Anti-Abortion Law”. A few things wrong here. Calling it a 5-3 ruling misses some important distinctions here. Lets go to the actual opinion of the court

Justice Thomas was the one one who would of straight up upheld the Texas abortion law.The dissent of Alito and Roberts wanted to remand to a lower court based on procedural reasons, but pretty clearly would have struck down at least part of the law. And the core of Roe v. Wade was not in serious doubt here. Roe is effectively settled law at this point. Only justice Thomas has ever expressed serious interest in overturning it. (See Fogg and Greene’s book Weged for a source on this)

The Black Lives Matter protesters are going to…

The Black Lives Matter protesters are going to be “disruptive” at christmas eve shopping today.

http://www.startribune.com/mall-of-america-prepares-for-black-lives-matter-protest/363386781/
http://archive.is/37LQ9

There is a strong case for police reform. However, this is a remarkably counterproductive strategy. They are targeting businesses and shoppers that have close to nothing to do with their objectives. If any actual policy reform is to happen, the police reform movement needs to convince moderates that the issue is worth concern. This type of disruptive protest only serves to anger moderates. A traditionally organized political group would not have done something like this.

Really, the whole Black Lives Matter movement shows the limits of internet activism. Traditional meatspace political and activist groups are effective because they can set clear objectives, they can pick their battles, and because they can control the more extreme members of their group.

Twitter hashtag activism has none of those luxuries. And, particularly when that activism and discourse happens on the internet, good discourse is driven out by bad discourse. So, in the Twitter hashtag world, social justice activism gets taken over by click bait, jerks, and self-pleasuring trolls
